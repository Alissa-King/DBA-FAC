About this project — click to expand or collapse
What is the FAC? The Federal Audit Clearinghouse (FAC) is the government database of Single Audit reports — independent annual audits required for any organization spending $750,000 or more of federal funds in a fiscal year.
What does this project ask? Can we predict which organizations will receive audit findings before their audit closes, using only information already on file? If yes, the model supports risk-based triage and capacity planning.
Why is the test a future year? Models train on earlier years and are tested on a future year they never saw — the same way a real screening tool operates. A random split would let the model "see the future" and inflate scores.
What is SHAP? SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) is a method for explaining individual predictions. Instead of just saying "this model uses low-risk status," SHAP tells us exactly how much that fact pushed a specific organization's risk score up or down.

The FAC pilot uses the included 2022–2023 bounded pull. Walkthrough artifact, not final dissertation evidence.
